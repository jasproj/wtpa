What to Bring on a Key West Fishing Charter

✍️ Captain Dane Karcher 📅 April 2026 ⏱️ 7 min read

One of the best parts about booking a charter with Captain Dane is that we provide most of the heavy lifting. All fishing tackle, reels, rods, and tackle bags are included. But there are still essentials you need to bring for comfort, safety, and an amazing experience. Here's exactly what to pack.

The Great News: What We Provide

Captain Dane's all-inclusive charters come equipped with:

  • Professional fishing rods and reels
  • All tackle (lures, weights, hooks, line)
  • Tackle bags and organization
  • Cooler with ice
  • Safety equipment (life jackets, first aid)
  • Boat cover for sun protection
  • Navigation and fish-finding electronics

This is different from many charters that nickel-and-dime you for equipment. We believe in all-inclusive experiences.

Essentials You Must Bring

☀️ Sun & Heat Protection

  • Reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+) — Reapply every 2 hours. The water reflects UV rays, doubling exposure.
  • Polarized sunglasses — Reduces glare, lets you spot fish. Game-changer.
  • Hat or visor — Protects scalp and face
  • Light long-sleeve shirt — Protects arms from sun and fish spines

💧 Hydration & Nutrition

  • 2+ liters of water (bring your own bottle; we have ice)
  • Electrolyte drinks (coconut water, Gatorade)
  • Snacks (granola bars, fruit, nuts, sandwiches)
  • Lunch or prefer we stop? Communicate before the charter

🎣 Personal Fishing Gear (Optional)

  • Your favorite fishing glove — Many anglers have a lucky glove
  • Personal polarized glasses — Some prefer specific lenses
  • Your own tackle — If you have trusted lures or hooks

📷 Photography & Recording

  • Waterproof phone case — Essential for photos and videos
  • GoPro or action camera (optional) — Capture the action
  • Camera with strap — Keep it secure on deck

🏥 Health & Safety

  • Seasickness medication — Take 30 minutes before departure if prone
  • Any personal medications
  • Insect repellent — For dock time or evening charters

For Overnight or Multi-Day Charters

  • Change of clothes
  • Towel (bring your own; we have some)
  • Sleeping bag or blanket (if sleeping on the boat)
  • Toiletries
  • Any medications or medical supplies

For Spearfishing Charters

If you're booking a spearfishing or freediving charter, bring:

  • Your wetsuit (if you own one; we have rentals)
  • Diving mask (if you prefer your own prescription lens)
  • Fins (personal preference for fit)
  • Gloves (coral protection and warmth)

What NOT to Bring

  • Heavy luggage: Space is limited on the boat. Pack light.
  • Glass bottles: Safety hazard. Use plastic or cans only.
  • Metal tackle boxes: They rust and corrode in salt spray. Use plastic.
  • Excessive alcohol: You can bring beer or wine, but we need you alert and safe.
  • Loose electronics: They'll get wet. Use waterproof cases.
  • Strong perfume/cologne: Fish smell it, and it bothers other guests.

Pro Packing Tips from Captain Dane

  • Compression packing: Use a dry bag to keep everything organized and protected from salt spray
  • Label your gear: Use waterproof stickers or tape with your name
  • Waterproof everything: Phone, wallet, keys—all go in waterproof cases
  • Arrive early: Give yourself 30 minutes before departure to settle in and ask questions
  • Dress in layers: Early morning is cool; afternoon sun is hot
  • Break in your gear: Don't wear brand-new shoes or sunglasses for the first time on the charter

The Charter Day Timeline

Meet at the dock 30 minutes early. We'll cover safety briefings, go over the day's plan, and get you comfortable on the boat. Most charters are 4–8 hours, so plan your breakfast accordingly. Bring snacks or eat before boarding.

Pack your personal items in a small dry bag or backpack. You'll have a safe place to store it while fishing.

Charter Dress Code

There's no formal dress code, but practicality wins:

  • Comfortable, quick-dry clothes
  • Non-slip shoes (water or boat shoes)
  • Avoid loose jewelry or watches (can get caught in fishing line)
  • Consider a light long-sleeve shirt for sun protection and fish spine safety

Money, Tips & Valuables

Bring minimal cash. Most charters process payment before or after via card. If you're tipping Captain Dane (and you should—great service deserves it), cash is appreciated. Plan 15–20% if it was an excellent experience.

Leave valuables (expensive watches, rings) at your hotel. The ocean is unpredictable, and items can be lost.
